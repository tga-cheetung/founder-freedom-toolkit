## Why Default Hiring Advice Fails

Without guidance, AI gives hiring advice that:

1. **Skips the prerequisites.** "You should hire an SDR" without checking
   whether a playbook, pipeline, or onboarding plan exists. The hire shows
   up to an empty desk.

2. **Gives generic role descriptions.** "Hire a salesperson" is not a
   recommendation. "Hire a junior SDR to qualify inbound and set demos,
   $50-65K base, someone who's sold $10-30K deals in a technical space" is.

3. **Ignores economics.** A $120K OTE hire with 6 months ramp time is a
   $100K+ bet before they close a single deal. Most founders at $1-3M ARR
   haven't done this math.

4. **Defaults to "hire senior."** Senior hires are more autonomous but cost
   2-3x and often won't follow a playbook they didn't build. At this stage,
   a junior who executes a documented process often outperforms a senior
   who wants to "do it my way."

5. **Doesn't distinguish role types.** Marketing hire vs. sales hire vs.
   ops hire solve completely different problems. The right first hire depends
   on where the bottleneck actually is â€” not what sounds good on LinkedIn.

## Anti-Patterns

**Always saying "not ready."** Some founders genuinely are ready. If they
have a documented process, consistent pipeline, clear role definition, and
the budget â€” tell them to hire NOW and give a strong recommendation. Being
permanently cautious is as harmful as being premature.

**Generic prerequisites.** "You need a documented process" is obvious.
"You need your discovery questions and objection handling written down so
a junior rep can run a first call without you" is useful. Be specific to
THEIR situation.

**Vague role recommendations.** "Hire a salesperson" isn't advice. "Hire a
junior SDR at $55-65K base to qualify inbound leads and book demos. Look for
someone who's done 50+ cold calls/day at a SaaS company selling $10-30K
deals. They don't need to close â€” they need to follow the process" is advice.

**Ignoring the economics.** "You should hire a senior AE" to a founder at
$1M ARR is irresponsible. Do the math out loud: "$130K OTE + 6 months ramp
= you're investing $100K before they close a deal. At your ARR, that's 10%
of revenue. Can you carry that?"

**Recommending "hire a VA" as a first GTM hire.** A VA is not a GTM hire.
If the founder needs admin help, say so â€” but don't dress it up as solving
the GTM problem. Admin delegation is a task; GTM hiring is a strategy.

**Skipping the 30/60/90.** If the founder can't describe what success looks
like at 30, 60, and 90 days, neither can the hire. This is the single
biggest predictor of first-hire failure and should always be in the checklist.
